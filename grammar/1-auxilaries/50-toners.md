# Toners

These are the "small" Dutch words that translate badly in a dictionary but appear constantly in everyday speech.

On their own they carry almost no weight ‚Äî their job is to **set the tone**: soften, reassure, argue, hurry, or downplay. A Dutch speaker without them sounds robotic; with them, human.

> Learn these not from a dictionary but as ready-made stacked templates. Individually they're nearly weightless; together they carry the entire emotional register of spoken Dutch.

## toch

Modal particle. Core meaning: **"still / anyway / after all"** ‚Äî contrast or gentle insistence.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Against expectation | Het is duur, maar ik koop het `toch`. | It's expensive, but I'll buy it anyway. |
| Seeking confirmation | Je komt `toch`? | You're coming, right? |
| Mild reproach / reminder | Dat had ik `toch` gezegd! | I did say that, you know! |

Roughly: *still / after all / right / anyway*, depending on context.

## even

Two distinct senses ‚Äî don't mix them up.

**A. Particle "briefly / quickly / just" (most common)** ‚Äî softens a request, reduces the "weight" of an action (~90%).

- *Wacht `even`.* ‚Äî Wait a sec.
- *Kun je me `even` helpen?* ‚Äî Can you give me a quick hand?
- *Ik ga `even` naar de winkel.* ‚Äî I'll just pop to the shop.

**B. Adjective/adverb "equally / `even` (number)"**

- *`even` groot* ‚Äî equally big
- *een `even` getal* ‚Äî an `even` number

> `effe` is just a casual spoken form of `even`.

## nog

Adverb: **"still / yet / more"**.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Ongoing state | Ben je er nog? | Are you still there? |
| Addition | Nog een biertje, alsjeblieft. | One more beer, please. |
| `nog niet` | Ik ben nog niet klaar. | I'm not ready yet. |
| `nog steeds` (emphatic) | Hij slaapt nog steeds. | He's still sleeping. |

## wel

Core function: **affirmative counter to a negation**. No exact English word ‚Äî closest is stressed "do/does" or "actually".

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Rebutting a negative (key!) | ‚Äî Je hebt het niet gedaan. ‚Äî Ik heb het **wel** gedaan! | ‚Äî You didn't do it. ‚Äî I **did** do it! |
| Softening / concession | Dat is **wel** waar. | That's actually true. |
| | Ik wil **wel**. | I don't mind. / Sure. |
| Contrast in a sentence | Bier heb ik niet, wijn **wel**. | No beer, but I do have wine. |

`wel` is essentially the "anti-*niet*" ‚Äî it always pulls toward "yes" against an expected "no".

## nou

Word of the same class: **"well / come on / oh"**.

- ***Nou**?* ‚Äî Well? / So?
- *Nou ja...* ‚Äî Well, anyway...
- *Schiet **nou** op!* ‚Äî Come on, hurry up!

## maar

**A.** Conjunction "but"

- *Klein maar fijn.* ‚Äî Small but nice.

**B.** Particle "just / go ahead / -" (softens a command)** ‚Äî removes tension, gives "permission".

- *Ga maar zitten.* ‚Äî Go ahead, sit down.
- *Zeg het maar.* ‚Äî Go on, tell me.
- *Laat maar.* ‚Äî Never mind / forget it. (very common!)
- *Begin maar.* ‚Äî Go ahead, start.

Flavor: "don't worry about it, just do it".

## hoor

Purely colloquial particle at the **end** of a sentence. Untranslatable as a single word ‚Äî it's warmth packed into a syllable.

Carries no meaning but sets the **tone**: friendliness, reassurance, warmth. Remove `hoor` and the sentence becomes drier and more formal.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Reassure | Het is goed, **hoor**. | It's fine, really, don't worry. |
| Soften a no/yes | Nee, **hoor**. | Oh no, not at all. (gentle no) |
| | Ja, **hoor**. | Yeah, sure. |
| Encourage | Je doet het goed, **hoor**. | You're doing well, you really are. |

## dus

The most "logical" of the set ‚Äî adverb/conjunction **"so / therefore / then"**. Expresses conclusion or consequence.

- *Het regent, **dus** ik blijf thuis.* ‚Äî It's raining, so I'm staying home.
- ***Dus**?* ‚Äî So? / And?
- ***Dus** je komt niet?* ‚Äî So you're not coming?
- Sentence-final filler (mild irritation): *Ik heb geen tijd, **dus**.* ‚Äî I've got no time, so there.

## gewoon

**A.** Adjective "ordinary / plain"

- *een gewone dag* ‚Äî an ordinary day
- *gewone mensen* ‚Äî ordinary people

**B.** Particle "just / simply"** ‚Äî removes importance, says "without fuss".

- *Doe het **gewoon**.* ‚Äî Just do it.
- *Ik vind het **gewoon** leuk.* ‚Äî I just like it (no reason).
- *Het is **gewoon** kapot.* ‚Äî It's just broken.
- *Zeg het **gewoon**.* ‚Äî Just say it.

Flavor: "don't overcomplicate, it's just like this".

## eens

Particle softening a command, adding a **"-/once/sometime"** nuance.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Softening a command | Kom **eens** hier. | Come here (gently). |
| a sec | Luister **eens**. | Listen a sec. |
| "sometime / one day" | Je moet **eens** langskomen. | You should drop by sometime. |
| Fairy-tale opener | Er was **eens**... | Once upon a time... |

> often reduced to `'ns` / `es`

### `even` vs `eens`

`even` is about **brevity** ("not for long"); `eens` is about **softening the tone / vagueness** ("go on", "sometime").

| Dutch | English | Nuance |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Kom **`even`**. | Come for a moment. | emphasizes: briefly |
| Kom **eens**. | Come here (do). | emphasizes: softened request |
| Kom **eens `even`**. | Come over for just a sec. | both at once |

## net

**A. `net` = "just / just now" (time)** ‚Äî the recent past, seconds or minutes ago.

- *Ik ben **net** thuis.* ‚Äî I just got home.
- *Hij is **net** weg.* ‚Äî He just left.
- *De trein is **net** vertrokken.* ‚Äî The train just left.

Clean contrast with `even`:

| Dutch | English | Time |
|-------|---------|------|
| Ik ga **even** weg. | I'll step out briefly. | future, short |
| Ik ben **net** weg(gegaan). | I just left. | past, recent |

**B. `net` = "exactly / just / right"** ‚Äî emphasis on a precise match.

- ***Net** op tijd.* ‚Äî Just in time.
- ***Net** wat ik nodig heb.* ‚Äî Exactly what I need.
- *Dat is het **net**.* ‚Äî That's exactly the point.

Fixed constructions:

- **`net als`** ‚Äî just like / same as ‚Üí *Net als gisteren.* ‚Äî Just like yesterday.
- **`net zo ... als`** ‚Äî just as ... as ‚Üí *Zij is **net zo** slim **als** hij.* ‚Äî She's just as smart as he is.
- **`net niet`** ‚Äî almost / just barely not ‚Üí *Ik haalde het **net niet**.* ‚Äî I just barely missed it.

**C. `net` as adjective = "neat / tidy / decent"** (same form, different feel).

- *een **nette** jongen* ‚Äî a well-behaved boy
- ***nette** kleren* ‚Äî decent clothes
- *Doe **netjes**!* ‚Äî Behave yourself! (`netjes` = adverb form)

## wat

Besides the question word "what", `wat` works as a particle **"a bit / somewhat"** ‚Äî softens an adjective, downplays a quantity.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Soften an adjective | Het is `wat` koud. | It's a bit cold. |
| Downplay a quantity | Ik heb `wat` geld. | I've got some money. |
| Exclamation "how / what a" | `Wat` een mooie dag! | What a lovely day! |
| Question word | `Wat` zeg je? | What did you say? |

Flavor (as particle): "a touch, not too much" ‚Äî milder than *een beetje*.

## best

Besides the superlative "best", `best` works as a particle.

**A. Degree "quite / rather / pretty"** ‚Äî turns an adjective up a notch, less strong than *heel*.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Soften/scale an adjective | Het is `best` duur. | It's quite expensive. |
| | Dat was `best` moeilijk. | That was pretty hard. |
| | Ze is `best` aardig. | She's rather nice. |

**B. Willingness "sure / I don't mind"** ‚Äî like *wel*, agrees without enthusiasm.

- *Ik wil `best` helpen.* ‚Äî I'm happy enough to help.
- *Dat kan `best`.* ‚Äî That's quite possible / that's fine.
- *‚Äî Koffie? ‚Äî `Best`.* ‚Äî Coffee? ‚Äî Sure, why not.

Flavor: "more than you'd think, but no big deal".

## eigenlijk

Reframing particle: **"actually / really / when you think about it"**. Reopens a question or gently corrects an assumption.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Get to the real point | Wat wil je `eigenlijk`? | What do you actually want? |
| Mild correction | Dat klopt `eigenlijk` niet. | That's not really right. |
| Sudden realization | Ik heb `eigenlijk` best honger. | I'm actually pretty hungry. |

Flavor: "if we're honest / underneath it all".

## zeker

Two senses ‚Äî an adverb of certainty *and* a questioning particle.

**A. Adverb "certainly / surely"**

- *Dat weet ik `zeker`.* ‚Äî I know that for sure.

**B. Particle "I bet / by any chance"** ‚Äî voices a strong guess, often mildly teasing.

- *Jij bent `zeker` de nieuwe?* ‚Äî You must be the new one, right?
- *Je hebt `zeker` geen tijd?* ‚Äî I don't suppose you've got time?

Flavor (as particle): "I'm assuming, correct me if wrong".

## soms

Literally "sometimes", but as a particle in **questions** it means **"by any chance"** ‚Äî softens and makes the question tentative.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Tentative question | Heb je `soms` een pen? | Got a pen, by any chance? |
| Polite probing | Weet jij `soms` waar hij is? | Do you happen to know where he is? |
| Literal "sometimes" | Ik ga `soms` zwemmen. | I sometimes go swimming. |

Flavor (as particle): "no pressure if not".

## al

Adverb **"already"**, also a lightener/emphasis particle.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| "already" | Ben je er `al`? | Are you there already? |
| Scaling down | Zo moeilijk is het `al` niet. | It's not *that* hard. |
| Memory-jog (`al weer`) | Hoe heet hij `al` weer? | What was his name again? |

## zo

Besides "so/thus", `zo` marks the **very near future**: **"in a sec / shortly"**.

- *Ik kom `zo`.* ‚Äî I'll be right there.
- *We beginnen `zo`.* ‚Äî We're starting in a moment.
- *Tot `zo`!* ‚Äî See you in a bit!

> Contrast with `net` (just now, past) ‚Äî `zo` points forward, `net` points back.

## pas

Adverb **"only just / not until"** ‚Äî emphasizes that something is later or more recent than expected.

- *Hij komt `pas` morgen.* ‚Äî He won't come until tomorrow.
- *Ik ben `pas` net thuis.* ‚Äî I only just got home.
- *Het is `pas` acht uur.* ‚Äî It's only eight o'clock.

Flavor: "later / less than you'd think" ‚Äî the mirror of `al` ("already").

## lekker

Beyond "tasty", `lekker` is a strong **attitude particle** ‚Äî comfort, indulgence, or cheeky defiance.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Cosy indulgence | Ik ga `lekker` slapen. | I'm gonna have a nice sleep. |
| Defiant / "and I don't care" | Ik doe het `lekker` toch. | I'm doing it anyway, so there. |
| Sarcasm | Dat is `lekker` handig‚Ä¶ | Well, *that's* convenient‚Ä¶ |

Flavor: "and I'm enjoying it / and you can't stop me".

## gerust

Reassuring particle **"feel free / safely / with no worry"** ‚Äî gives permission and removes hesitation.

- *Ga `gerust` zitten.* ‚Äî Do feel free to sit.
- *Vraag het `gerust`.* ‚Äî Don't hesitate to ask.
- *Je kunt me `gerust` bellen.* ‚Äî You can absolutely call me.

Flavor: warmer, stronger cousin of `maar` ("go ahead").

## juist

Adverb/particle **"precisely / exactly / on the contrary"** ‚Äî sharpens rather than softens.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Emphatic agreement | `Juist`! | Exactly! |
| Contradiction | `Juist` niet! | Quite the opposite! |
| Pinpointing | `Juist` daarom. | For that very reason. |

Note: unlike most toners here, `juist` **adds** weight instead of removing it.

## echt

Intensifier **"really / genuinely / truly"** ‚Äî underlines sincerity or degree.

- *Dat is `echt` waar.* ‚Äî That's really true.
- *Ik meen het `echt`.* ‚Äî I really mean it.
- *`Echt`?* ‚Äî Really? / Seriously?
- *Het is `echt` niet mijn schuld.* ‚Äî It's genuinely not my fault.

Flavor: "no exaggeration, I mean this".

## erg

Two senses ‚Äî an everyday intensifier *and* an adjective "bad".

**A. Adverb "very / really"** ‚Äî plain degree booster, interchangeable with *heel* in most cases.

- *Het is `erg` duur.* ‚Äî It's very expensive.
- *Ik vind het `erg` leuk.* ‚Äî I really like it.
- *Niet zo `erg` groot.* ‚Äî Not all that big.

**B. Adjective "bad / serious"** ‚Äî how much something *matters*.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| "that's terrible" | Wat `erg`! | How awful! |
| "does it matter?" | Is het `erg`? | Is it bad? / Does it matter? |
| Waving it off | Niet `erg`, hoor. | No harm done. / Doesn't matter. |

> Watch the contrast: *`erg` goed* = "very good", but *Dat is `erg`* = "that's bad". The adverb boosts, the adjective condemns.

## The "just" trap

 English "just" covers three different Dutch words ‚Äî the main pitfall:

| English "just" | Dutch | Meaning |
|----------------|-------|---------|
| just = quickly | **even** | briefly, for a moment |
| just = simply | **gewoon** | plainly, without fuss |
| just = a moment ago | **net** | just now |
| just = exactly | **net** | precisely, right |

- *Just do it* ‚Üí **Doe het gewoon.**
- *Just a sec* ‚Üí **Even.**
- *I just got here* ‚Üí **Ik ben er net.**

## Stacking

The hallmark of fluent, natural speech is **stacking** these particles.

 A native speaker throws 2‚Äì3 together without thinking, and that's what gives a sentence its living, non-blunt tone.

- *Ga maar `even` zitten, hoor.* ‚Äî Do sit down for a moment, no worries. (`maar` + `even` + `hoor`)
- *Het is `toch` wel waar.* ‚Äî It really is true after all. (`toch` + `wel`)
- *Doe het nog maar `even`.* ‚Äî Just do it once more quickly. (`nog` + `maar` + `even`)
- *Doe het gewoon `even`.* ‚Äî Just do it real quick. (`gewoon` + `even`)
- *Kom maar eens `even` kijken, hoor.* ‚Äî Do come take a look sometime, go on. (four particles!)
- *Dat is dus `toch` wel zo.* ‚Äî So it really is the case after all. (`dus` + `toch` + `wel`)
- *Zeg het nou maar gewoon.* ‚Äî Oh just say it already. (`nou` + `maar` + `gewoon`)
