# Specifiers: Demostratives and determiners  

## Demostrative pronouns

Dutch distinguishes **near** vs. **far** and **de-words** vs. **het-words**.

| | de-word | he**t**-word | English |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| Near | **deze** | di**t** | this |
| Far | **die** | da**t** | that |

Examples:

- *deze man* — this man (de man, near)
- *dit kind* — this child (het kind, near)
- *die vrouw* — that woman (de vrouw, far)
- *dat huis* — that house (het huis, far)

### Demostrative pronouns as standalone references

*die* and *dat* are also used to refer back to a previously mentioned noun:

- *Ik zag een film. Die was geweldig.* (I saw a film. That one was great.)
- *Hij kocht een huis. Dat staat in Amsterdam.* (He bought a house. It's in Amsterdam.)

## degene

`Degene` / `diegene` s a demonstrative pronoun that is used to refer to a person or persons, without the gender of that person being known or relevant.

It literally means "the person who" or "the one who". It always refers to a person and is, as a rule, followed by a subordinate clause beginning with 'die' or by a phrase with a preposition.

Enkelvoud: "Degene die het laatst vertrekt, doet het licht uit."
Meervoud: "Degenen die nog geen kaartje hebben, kunnen achter aansluiten."

## zulk / zulke (such — "like that")

These are **qualitative demonstratives**: instead of pointing at *which* noun (like *deze / die*), they point at *what kind* of noun. English "such" or "like that".

`zulk` vs `zulke` follows the normal adjective-ending rule:

| Form | Used before | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **zulke** | de-words + all plurals | **Zulke** situaties leer je van. |
| **zulk** | singular het-words (mostly uncountable) | Bij **zulk** weer blijf ik binnen.|

- *Leer je van zulke situaties?* — Do you learn from situations like that?
- *Ik heb nog nooit zulke mensen ontmoet.* — I've never met people like that.
- *Zulk lekker eten!* — Such delicious food!

### zo'n (such a / like that — singular)

`zo'n` is the singular-countable counterpart of *zulke*. It is a contraction of **zo een** and is always followed by a singular noun.

- *Ik wil ook zo'n auto.* — I want a car like that too.
- *Zo'n dag vergeet je nooit.* — You never forget a day like that.
- *Heb je ooit zo'n man ontmoet?* — Have you ever met a man like that?

> **Rule of thumb:** *zo'n* + singular noun → plural becomes *zulke*.
> *zo'n huis* → *zulke huizen*; *zo'n idee* → *zulke ideeën*.

### dergelijk / dergelijke (such, similar — formal)

A more formal synonym of *zulk(e)*, common in written and official language. Same de/het ending rule.

- *Dergelijke problemen komen vaker voor.* — Such problems occur more often.
- *In een dergelijk geval bel je de politie.* — In a case like that you call the police.
- *soortgelijk / soortgelijke* — "similar", a close relative: *een soortgelijk product* (a similar product).

### Quantifying determiners (before a noun)

| Dutch | English | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **elk / elke** | each, every | **Elke** dag lees ik de krant. (Every day I read the newspaper.) |
| **ieder / iedere** | each, every | **Iedere** ochtend drink ik koffie. (Each morning I drink coffee.) |
| **alle** | all | **Alle** kinderen spelen buiten. (All the children play outside.) |
| **sommige** | some (certain ones) | **Sommige** mensen houden niet van kaas. (Some people don't like cheese.) |
| **enkele** | a few, some | **Enkele** studenten kwamen te laat. (A few students arrived late.) |
| **een paar** | a couple, a few | Ik heb **een paar** vragen. (I have a couple of questions.) |
| **meerdere** | several | **Meerdere** landen deden mee. (Several countries took part.) |
| **beide** | both | **Beide** kinderen zijn ziek. (Both children are ill.) |
| **alleen** | only one  | Hij is  **alleen** |
| **veel** | many, much | **Veel** mensen werken thuis. (Many people work from home.) |
| **weinig** | few, little | **Weinig** mensen weten dat. (Few people know that.) |
| **enige** | only | Hij is de **enige** die het weet. (He is the only one who knows.) |
| **beetje** | a few, a little  |  |
| **geen** | no, not any | Ik heb **geen** tijd. (I have no time.) |
| **veel** | many / much | Er zijn **veel** mensen op het feest. (There are many people at the party.) |
