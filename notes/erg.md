Two senses — an everyday intensifier *and* an adjective "bad".

**A. Adverb "very / really"** — plain degree booster, interchangeable with *heel* in most cases.

- *Het is `erg` duur.* — It's very expensive.
- *Ik vind het `erg` leuk.* — I really like it.
- *Niet zo `erg` groot.* — Not all that big.

**B. Adjective "bad / serious"** — how much something *matters*.

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| "that's terrible" | Wat `erg`! | How awful! |
| "does it matter?" | Is het `erg`? | Is it bad? / Does it matter? |
| Waving it off | Niet `erg`, hoor. | No harm done. / Doesn't matter. |

> Watch the contrast: *`erg` goed* = "very good", but *Dat is `erg`* = "that's bad". The adverb boosts, the adjective condemns.
