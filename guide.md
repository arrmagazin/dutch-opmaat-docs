# User guide

Welcome to **Taalmoed** — a free app to practise Dutch at level A2. This guide explains what each part of the app does and how to use it. Open a section below to read more.

Everything you need is on the home page: each card is one feature. Your progress is saved automatically in your own browser, so you can stop and continue any time.

## Getting started

1. On the home page, click a card to open a feature.
2. Use the sidebar on the left to jump between features at any time.
3. There is no login. Your progress and settings stay on your device.

> Tip: the app also works offline once it has loaded. Nothing you do is shared with other people.

## Theory

The grammar reference. Explanations of Dutch grammar with clear examples and tables.

- Browse topics in the sidebar tree, from simpler to harder.
- Click a section heading to open or close it.
- Use the expand and collapse buttons at the top of a page to open everything at once.

## Topics

Short thematic pages that group words and phrases around one everyday subject, so you learn vocabulary in context.

## Tutor

An AI conversation partner you can **talk to out loud**.

1. Press the microphone and start speaking in Dutch.
2. When you stop, the tutor understands you, replies in text, and reads the answer aloud.
3. It then listens again — so you get a natural, hands-free conversation.

> You need to allow the browser to use your microphone. Speech recognition happens in your browser; your audio is not stored.

## Writing

Practise short written Dutch and get instant AI feedback.

- Write a short text on the given task.
- The app checks it and shows corrections, with your words and the suggested words highlighted so you can see the difference.

## Videos

Watch Dutch videos with subtitles.

- Filter by level and search for a video by title.
- Watched videos are marked as completed.

## Vocabulary

Word lists Dutch–English, grouped by theme.

- Click a word to hear it spoken.
- Show or hide the translation while you practise.

## Strong verbs

Irregular (strong) verbs and their conjugations.

- Study them in a table, test yourself with a quiz, or drill them as flashcards.

## Flashcards

Learn words with flashcards that use **spaced repetition**: cards you find hard come back sooner, cards you know come back later. Your review schedule is saved automatically.

## Questions

A hands-free question-and-answer drill.

1. The app asks a question out loud.
2. You answer by speaking.
3. It listens for your spoken answer and moves on.

Good for practising quick, natural responses without typing.

## Time planner

Track how much time you spend practising. The planner shows your activity per day so you can build a steady learning habit.

## External links

A curated list of other good places to learn Dutch — websites, YouTube channels and books — grouped by type.

## Your progress and settings

- **Progress** (finished sections, watched videos, flashcard schedule) is stored locally in your browser.
- Open **Settings** from the button at the bottom of the sidebar to adjust preferences.
- Because everything is stored on your device, clearing your browser data will also clear your progress.

## Tips

- Practise a little every day — the time planner helps you keep the habit.
- Use the tutor and questions features to practise speaking, not just reading.
- Turn new words from the vocabulary and topics pages into flashcards to remember them longer.
